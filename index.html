What is the cash ratio?
The cash ratio is a measurement of the firm's liquidity. It measures the ratio of a firm's total cash and cash equivalents to it's current liabilites.
How to calculate cash ratio?
To calculate cash ratio you should use the following formula:
cash ratio = (cash + cash equivalent) / total current liabilities
Example: Company x has $35,000,000 in cash, $12,000,000 worth of cash equivalent and it also has $70,000,000 in total current liabilities, calculate the cash ratio?
Using the cash ratio formula the answer will be:
cash ratio = (cash + cash equivalent) / total current liabilities
cash ratio = ($35,000,000 + $12,000,000) / $70,000,000
cash ratio = 0.67
